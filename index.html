Strategy Framework

Classic strategic frameworks for business analysis

Porter's Five Forces

Analyze industry competition environment

How to Use:

  1. Identify suppliers and their bargaining power
  2. Analyze buyer bargaining power
  3. Assess threat of new entrants
  4. Evaluate threat of substitutes
  5. Analyze competitive rivalry

SWOT Analysis

Strategic planning technique for strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, threats

How to Use:

  1. List internal strengths
  2. Identify internal weaknesses
  3. Find external opportunities
  4. Recognize external threats
  5. Develop strategies based on analysis

PEST Analysis

Macro-environmental analysis framework

How to Use:

  1. Analyze Political factors
  2. Evaluate Economic factors
  3. Assess Social factors
  4. Review Technological factors

BCG Matrix

Product portfolio analysis tool

How to Use:

  1. Identify Stars (high growth, high share)
  2. Find Cash Cows (low growth, high share)
  3. Recognize Question Marks (high growth, low share)
  4. Identify Dogs (low growth, low share)

Thinking Framework

Powerful frameworks for structured thinking and problem solving

First Principles Thinking

Break down complex problems into basic elements

How to Apply:

  1. Identify and define your current assumptions
  2. Break down the problem into its fundamental principles
  3. Create new solutions from scratch
  4. Test and validate your new approach

MECE Principle

Mutually Exclusive, Collectively Exhaustive

How to Apply:

  1. Break down problems into mutually exclusive categories
  2. Ensure all possibilities are covered
  3. Avoid overlaps between categories
  4. Use for comprehensive problem analysis

SCAMPER Technique

Creative thinking tool for innovation

How to Apply:

  1. Substitute - What can be replaced?
  2. Combine - What can be merged?
  3. Adapt - What can be adjusted?
  4. Modify - What can be changed?
  5. Put to another use - New applications?
  6. Eliminate - What can be removed?
  7. Reverse - What can be reversed?

Six Thinking Hats

Parallel thinking method for better decisions

How to Apply:

  1. White Hat - Facts and information
  2. Red Hat - Emotions and feelings
  3. Black Hat - Caution and criticism
  4. Yellow Hat - Benefits and optimism
  5. Green Hat - Creativity and alternatives
  6. Blue Hat - Process control
